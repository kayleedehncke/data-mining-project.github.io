Project Proposal

Proposal Overview

Research Topic

This project focuses on analyzing extreme heat events across the United States to better understand how these events vary across geographic regions and change over time. By examining historical temperature patterns, the project aims to identify spatial and temporal trends in extreme heat and their broader implications for public health and climate resilience.

Scope

The scope of this project includes a nationwide analysis of historical extreme temperature conditions across the United States. The analysis emphasizes regional variation and long-term trends while remaining focused on descriptive and comparative patterns present in observed climate data.

Goals

The primary goal of this project is to identify where and when extreme heat events occur most frequently across the United States. Additional goals include comparing regional exposure to extreme heat, exploring associations between heat events and disaster-related impacts, and examining patterns that may indicate increased risk of extreme temperature days.

Research Questions

  1. How has the frequency of extreme heat days changed across the United States over the past several decades?
  2. Which regions of the United States experience the highest number of extreme heat events?
  3. How do extreme heat events vary seasonally across different geographic regions?
  4. What long-term trends are observable in the intensity of extreme heat events over time?
  5. How do extreme heat patterns differ between urban and rural regions?
  6. Are certain states or climate regions disproportionately affected by extreme heat events?
  7. How do years with major heat-related disaster declarations compare to years without such declarations?
  8. What environmental and geographic factors are most strongly associated with the occurrence of extreme heat days?
  9. Can historical weather patterns be used to estimate the likelihood of future extreme heat events in specific regions?
  10. Are there identifiable patterns that precede periods of unusually high extreme heat activity?
